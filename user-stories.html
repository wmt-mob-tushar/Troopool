User Stories, Goal & Market

How real users use Troopool, the goal, and the primary market.

Updated for v4 (networking-first). Companion to BUSINESS-ANALYSIS.md and FLOW-AUDIT.md.

1. The goal (one paragraph)

Troopool is NOT a carpool app — it's a networking platform for professionals who happen to commute together. The commute is the reason to meet; the network is the product. Same route, same office corridor, same interests — Troopool turns the people already in your daily orbit into connections, carpools, circles and a real professional community. Positioning: LinkedIn × sRide (not Uber × sRide). The first screen is People in your orbit, never "Find a ride."

The winning loop: Route match → swipe to connect → match → chat → carpool → friendship → circle → community → daily engagement.

Success = a verified professional who, within 2 weeks, has made real connections (swiped into matches), carpooled at least once, and joined a circle — and keeps opening the app daily.

2. Primary market

Primary (beachhead): verified corporate professionals in Indian IT corridors.
- Who: 25–40-year-old professionals at large campuses (Infosys, TCS, Wipro, Cognizant…), commuting 15–25 km daily in Pune, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Mumbai, Delhi-NCR.
- Why them first: dense, predictable corridors (easy matching), captive via employer rollout (solves cold-start), high cab spend (savings hook), and acute hybrid-work loneliness (lost the office as a social/professional layer).
- Buyer (B2B): HR / facilities / sustainability — for retention, engagement, parking relief and BRSR Scope-3 reporting.

Secondary: newcomers to a city, hybrid workers wanting a professional circle, and women seeking a safer, verified commute.

Geography: India-first; Maharashtra (legal since Apr 2025) + Bengaluru-corporate as the launch wedge → 8–12 cities over 3 years.

4. How it works (the user's mental model)

  1. Welcome — "Meet professionals already in your daily orbit."
  2. Build your Trust Score — phone + work email (required); DigiLocker / selfie optional, each raising a visible 0–100 Trust Score. Higher trust = more & better matches. (No LinkedIn.)
  3. Set your orbit & interests — home/office, days, timing + professional interests (AI, Product, Engineering, Startups…).
  4. Network (the home of the app) — a Tinder-style swipe deck of people in your orbit: ✕ pass / ❤ connect. Mutual like → "It's a match!" → chat. Below it: browse by route / nearby / interests.
  5. Do something real — chat action chips (Coffee / Carpool / Walk / Event / Circle); carpool a match (host accepts → ride group chat → track → rate → recurring).
  6. Community — professional Circles (Pune AI, Hinjewadi Founders, Women in Tech…) with group chat, events, polls, carpool requests; meet other circles.
  7. Home + Profile — daily dashboard with notifications, today's commute, new matches, circle updates; profile shows your Trust Score, connections, and (for corporates) the HR/ESG dashboard.

5. User stories (As a … I want … so that …)

Trust & onboarding

  • As a new user, I want a visible Trust Score I can raise with DigiLocker/selfie, so that I look credible and get better matches — and only meet accountable, verified professionals.
  • As a woman, I want high-trust, verified profiles + women-only mode + SOS, so that connecting and carpooling feel safe.

Network (swipe-to-match — the core)

  • As any user, I want to swipe through people in my orbit (route / nearby / interests) and connect on a mutual like, so that meeting feels effortless — like sRide's "Meet People," not a formal request.
  • As a user, I want to see who liked me, so that I can connect back quickly.
  • As Aisha, I want "People you may know" to appear after a few connections, so that my network compounds from my commute.

Chat & carpool (the reason to meet)

  • As a match, I want chat action chips (Coffee / Carpool / Walk / Event / Circle), so that a connection turns into a real plan.
  • As Rahul (host), I want to accept riders and open a ride group chat where all co-passengers connect, so that the carpool builds my network, not just my fuel savings.
  • As a rider, I want the exact cost split before I commit and a 2-way rating after, so that there are no surprises and trust stays high.

Community

  • As Ankit, I want professional Circles with events, polls and carpool requests, so that I meet groups of peers (founders, PMs, AI builders), not just one person.
  • As a circle, I want to meet other circles (say hi → group chat), so that my community expands safely.

Daily engagement & corporate

  • As any user, I want a Home dashboard + smart notifications (morning route, new match, ride, event), so that I have a reason to open the app daily — without spam.
  • As Sahil (HR/ESG), I want a dashboard of adoption, new connections, community participation, an Employee Engagement Score, CO₂ (Scope-3) and parking saved, so that I can prove engagement, retention and BRSR impact.

6. End-to-end journeys (in the prototype)

  • A — Connect (primary): Welcome → Trust Score → orbit/interests → Network swipe → ❤ on a route-mate → It's a match! → chat → carpool → ride group → rate.
  • B — Community: Network → join Pune AI Circle → RSVP an event → vote a poll → offer a carpool request → meet another circle.
  • C — Daily: Home → morning notification → today's commute → new matches → circle update → coffee request.
  • D — Corporate: Profile → HR / ESG dashboard → adoption · new connections · engagement score 8.4/10 · CO₂ Scope-3 · ₹ savings.

7. What makes it different

  • People first, not rides — the first screen is your orbit, not a ride search.
  • Swipe-to-match (sRide-iOS style) — connecting feels effortless and mutual.
  • Trust Score — verified, accountable professionals; safety as a feature.
  • The commute compounds into a network — carpool → friendship → circle → community → daily habit.
  • B2B engagement & ESG — the corporate story carpool-only apps can't tell.
